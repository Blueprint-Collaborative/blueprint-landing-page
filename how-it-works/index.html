Our commitment

The three things we will never do.

01
We don't touch your money.

Blueprint never handles, processes, or intermediates any funds. Grants move directly from your DAF provider to recipient organizations through the same custodial channels they always have. We are not in that transaction.

02
We don't take a cut.

We charge Blueprint subscribers a platform fee — that's our business model, and it's transparent. We never take a percentage of grants, never charge recipient organizations, and never monetize the movement of community capital. Not now, not ever.

03
We don't hold your data hostage.

Your giving history, your relationships, your community connections — that's yours. We don't sell it, don't share it with third parties, and don't use it to train models or serve ads. If you leave Blueprint, you take your data with you.

The honest part

This industry has a trust problem. We're not pretending otherwise.

There have been platforms that promised to connect capital with community — and then took fees no one agreed to, shut down without warning and left organizations scrambling, or treated donor and community data as a product to be monetized.

We built Blueprint because we believe infrastructure for good shouldn't extract from the people it serves. But we also know that trust isn't claimed — it's demonstrated. So here is what we've built to back that up:

No fund custody, ever.

Blueprint is structurally incapable of holding funds. It's not a policy decision — it's how we're built.

DAF provider partnerships are formal and auditable.

Our integrations go through your provider's official channels, with full documentation.

Recipient organizations are never charged.

Nonprofits, cooperatives, and CDFIs on Blueprint pay nothing to be discoverable or to receive grants.

Our fee structure is public.

What you pay for Blueprint is on our pricing page. There are no hidden take rates.
